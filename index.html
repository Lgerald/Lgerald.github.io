I am a Fullstack software engineer currently in Brooklyn, NY. I recently graduated from the Grace Hopper program, and am Currently working as a teaching fellow. Before attending the Grace Hopper program, I followed the STEM pipeline, in the hopes of becoming a doctor. I’ve always been passionate about Healthcare, so much so that my motto is a variant of the hippocratic oath: “the first rule is do no harm, second is to protect privacy. After that, do everything humanly possible to take care of the people around you.” When I came near the end of my pre-med journey however, I had a sudden realization that I was more passionate about accessibility of healthcare, rather than patient interaction. I found myself more drawn to the systems and organization behind medical practices, so I began learning about computer science. I used MIT and Stanford open courses and before I knew it I was hooked. I find the parallels between natural and technological patterns to be very intriguing and I’m excited to learn more about new technologies, and how technology can continue to grow and empower people.
